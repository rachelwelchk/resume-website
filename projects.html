PRODUCT MARKETING ASSESSMENT DESCRIPTION:

You are a product manager who has managed the creation of a new high-tech travel coffee mug. The coffee mug was designed for on-the-go people, traveling for work, and other long adventures. The coffee mug that your team has created was designed to keep coffee at the perfect temperature for up to 8 hours. The mug reads the temperature and will heat itself up every half hour based on the rolling temperature. Consider the technology that may have gone into the travel mug and who would use a high-tech mug.

Now it is up to your team to market and send product messaging to the correct target audience. Consider the functionality and usability as expressed in the Value Matrix we discussed in the lesson, and how this will affect the market segments your team will be addressing. Before you can send out messaging, you must identify your target audience using an empathy map and developing personas to create the best story.

PRODUCT DESIGN ASSESSMENT DESCRIPTION:

You are a product manager at a startup that is developing tools for creators. You are working with a small design team with two dedicated designers assigned to this project. You also have three software developers who are working with your team. Your team uses Figma to collaborate for design iterations. Your team also uses Adobe Illustrator to create static assets.

Your goal in this portfolio assessment is to follow the end-to-end design process to come up with a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) to build. First, you have to come up with a vision and mission statement for your group. That vision feeds into your specific business and product goals. You also need to narrow down your focus to a specific user segment to start your initial iterations. After that, come up with a prioritized list of needs for those users. For a prioritized need, create three solutions to serve those needs. Out of those three solutions, choose one and build a prototype that can be tested with the initial set of users.
